How fast is Ibex?

Ibex against Polars, DuckDB, ClickHouse, DataFusion, data.table and SQLite on the same columnar queries, from 1M to 16M rows. Times are the mean of timed iterations; lower is better.

Threads. Ibex runs on a single core. Polars, DuckDB, DataFusion and ClickHouse use all 8 — so they win the parallelisable cells on core count, not per-core work. For a same-core comparison read Ibex against polars-st (Polars pinned to one thread); Ibex is faster on the large majority of queries there.

Caveats. Every engine now materialises its full result. tf rolling EWMA is time-windowed in Ibex versus full-series in Polars (both O(n), different maths). SQLite and the data.table rolling median/std cells are pinned from an earlier run (they are stable and dominate wall-clock).
